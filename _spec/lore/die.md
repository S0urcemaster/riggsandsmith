# The Dice

## Definition

The dice are real in-world components.

They are not symbolic UI elements and not ordinary gambling dice.

They are fate dice: rare future-era probability units that generate localized fields which influence surrounding systems and event outcomes.

## Nature

Each die is a complex unit with:

- a mechanical structure
- an internal energy behavior
- a field signature
- a probability bias
- a risk profile

Some dice are fully engineered.

Some are partially reconstructed from obscure plans, damaged originals, or black-market variants.

Some appear to behave in ways that are not fully explained by accepted science.

This is why they are treated as both technical devices and quasi-magical objects.

## Function Inside the Rig

Inside a hacking rig, dice act as modular probability processors.

They do not merely calculate odds.

They emit and shape a field that pushes nearby systems toward certain outcomes, distortions, repetitions, or anomalies.

This is why the player can influence:

- roll behavior
- payout tendencies
- event duplication
- instability
- chain reactions

## Interaction Logic

Dice can strengthen or weaken each other depending on type, arrangement, and operating state.

This interaction may produce:

- synergy
- interference
- amplification
- suppression
- corruption
- cascade behavior

A strong build is therefore never just a set of individually good dice.

It is a field composition that behaves well together under pressure.

## Readable World Rule

The visible dice form is important.

It is both:

- the physical format of the component
- the readable gameplay language of probability manipulation

The player should understand that the machine is built around real dice modules because those modules are the source of the probability field itself.

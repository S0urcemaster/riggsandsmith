# The Dice

## Definition

The dice are real components of the world.

They are not ordinary gambling dice.

They are fate dice: precisely manufactured units that, under specific operating conditions, generate a local field and thereby bend the probability distribution of a limited space by a small amount.

## Discovery

At some point it was discovered that certain dice geometries show unusual behavior when suspended in strong magnetic fields and driven to very high rotational speed.

Under these conditions, an effect emerges that cannot be described only as mechanical or electrical.

The die couples, in a limited way, to a deeper probabilistic order of reality.

That order can be described as the fate constant.

In its normal state, it distributes event possibility in a practically indifferent way, without visible favoritism.

The die does not bend that distribution in a large or dramatic way, but only slightly, locally, and under load.

That slight bend is nevertheless enough to measurably influence technical systems with many sensitive state transitions.

## Nature

Each die is a complex unit with at minimum:

- a precise body geometry
- a material signature
- an operating behavior under magnetic suspension
- a field signature
- a probability tendency
- a risk profile

Some dice are fully industrially manufactured.

Some are based on reconstructed plans, damaged originals, or black-market variants.

Some do not behave fully in line with what accepted science would expect.

This is why they are treated both as technical devices and as objects of almost occult significance.

## Field Effect

A fate die does not generate its effect at rest.

It must be magnetically suspended inside a suitable rig, held in stable control, and brought up to operating speed.

Under those conditions, several things arise at once:

- heat
- electrical overload
- mechanical strain
- field noise
- a local probability distortion

That distortion does not form a visible dome.

It is better understood as a small projected bubble in which the affected space is statistically bent.

Within that bubble, events are not freely rewritten.

They are only pushed slightly in certain directions.

## Function Inside the Rig

Inside a rig, dice operate as modular field sources and probability processors.

They do not merely calculate odds.

They generate and shape a field that pushes nearby systems toward certain outputs, repetitions, disruptions, or cascades.

This is why the player can influence, among other things:

- roll behavior
- payout tendencies
- event duplication
- instability
- chain reactions

## Interaction Logic

Dice can strengthen or disrupt each other depending on type, arrangement, magnetic guidance, and operating state.

This can produce, among other things:

- synergy
- interference
- amplification
- suppression
- corruption
- cascade behavior

A strong build is therefore not merely a set of individually good dice.

It is a field composition that remains stable together under load, or becomes deliberately unstable in a controlled way.

## Readability Rule

The visible dice form is important.

It is both:

- the physical format of the component
- the readable gameplay language of probability manipulation

The player should understand that the machine is built around real dice modules because those modules are the source of the field itself.

# Hack

## Definition

In lore terms, a hack is not just an illegal technical operation.

It is a state of extreme concentration in which Lucky Smith locks onto the rig and the target machine so completely that the surrounding world falls away.

## Perceptual State

When Lucky Smith hacks a machine, the outside world seems to disappear.

His attention collapses into a narrow operational tunnel containing only the rig, the target machine, and the changing state between them.

The room, the noise around him, and everything not immediately connected to the operation lose importance.

What remains is directed control under pressure.

## Time Pressure

This state is not calm in a detached sense.

It is fast, compressed, and unforgiving.

He must make judgments at millisecond speed while sustaining attention and focus across the whole operation.

The hack therefore feels less like broad strategic thinking and more like riding a dangerous live condition without losing control of it.

## Body Rule

The body matters during a hack.

Pulse, breathing, tension, and stress are not decorative sensations.

They shape whether control can be maintained.

If the pulse climbs too far or breathing slips out of control, mistakes become more likely.

For that reason, Lucky Smith learns to watch his breath and regulate himself while operating.

## Red Zone

One of the central fears during a hack is crossing into the red zone.

The red zone is the condition in which strain, instability, or overload pushes the operation toward failure.

It is not merely a number on a display.

It is the felt edge beyond which control begins to break down.

Avoiding that edge becomes a core survival instinct.

## World Meaning

A hack matters in the lore because it reveals the true cost of using the rig.

The machine does not simply grant illegal leverage.

It demands total attention, bodily control, and the willingness to stay inside a dangerous narrowing of perception where profit, error, and collapse sit very close together.

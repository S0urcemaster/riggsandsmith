# John Riggs / Lucky Smith

## Definition

John Riggs is not a wealthy elite operator.

He is an ordinary and poorly paid programmer.

His daytime life is unremarkable, constrained, and economically limited.

Lucky Smith is not a separate person.

It is the name John Riggs uses in his illegal hacking life.

## Day Life

In everyday life, John Riggs works a normal programming job.

He is technically capable, but not exceptional in status.

He does not live close to real power.

He experiences the future world from below:

- limited money
- repetitive work
- low recognition
- little control over his life

This part of his life explains his situation, but it is not itself the playable game loop.

## Motive

John Riggs gets involved with rig machines for two reasons:

- to supplement his income
- to escape boredom and reclaim a sense of agency

He is not driven by grand ideology.

He is drawn by curiosity, technical fascination, risk, and the thrill of pushing a system that is not supposed to be bendable.

## Night Life

After work, he returns home and devotes himself to his rigging hobby.

He tunes his machine, experiments with dice, and prepares illegal probability runs.

After that, he takes the rig out to target slot machines and similar systems for limited profit.

Then the cycle begins again.

## Tone Rule

John Riggs should feel close to the player.

He is not a master criminal fantasy from the start.

He is a normal person with technical skill, low leverage, and a dangerous hobby that can gradually pull him into a larger illegal world.

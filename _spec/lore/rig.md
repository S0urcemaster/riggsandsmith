# The Rig

## Definition

The rig is an illegal probability hacking machine built to host and drive fate dice.

It is not a normal computer and not a simple cheating tool.

It is a field manipulation platform that tunes, powers, and combines dice so their effects can be directed into a target system.

## Purpose

A rig exists to interfere with real machines, especially gambling machines, by forcing probability distortions into their local operating reality.

In practical terms, the rig lets the user:

- mount dice modules
- stabilize or overdrive their field behavior
- shape synergy between modules
- push output toward profit
- risk instability, exposure, and collapse

## Construction

Rigs are built from a mix of:

- advanced hardware
- improvised illegal modifications
- black-market power systems
- shielding and cooling parts
- forbidden or unstable dice interfaces

No fully legitimate market openly sells complete rigs.

They are assembled through underground knowledge, hidden suppliers, and stolen or leaked components.

## Plans and Network

Rig plans circulate through hidden networks comparable to a darknet.

These networks distribute:

- build plans
- wiring schemes
- dice housing standards
- illegal firmware
- casino target notes
- modified field-control modules

Some plans are reliable.

Some are traps, scams, sabotage, or half-understood copies.

This makes the underground rig scene powerful, secretive, and dangerous.

## World Rule

A functioning rig is never fully safe.

Even a well-built machine carries the risk of:

- instability
- feedback
- component damage
- traceable signatures
- catastrophic collapse

The player should understand that using a rig means operating a forbidden machine that bends luck at real personal risk.

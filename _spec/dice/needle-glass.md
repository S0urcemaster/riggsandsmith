## Lore Identity

Needle Glass is a cheap light-fast fate die.

It is thin, sharp, responsive, and easy to underestimate because it does not hit hard by itself.

Its field character is quick rather than strong.

Under load, Needle Glass reacts fast to small operating changes and can help a weak build catch short timing windows.

It is also fragile.

Operators treat it as an acceptable early lesson because breaking one hurts, but does not ruin a run.

The die teaches that not every useful die is durable, and that cheap power or speed can carry a repair, damage, or replacement cost.

Needle Glass should feel like the kind of part a small operator buys when they finally have enough money to make the second slot matter, but not enough money to buy real muscle.

## Product Identity

Needle Glass is the preferred first additional die for early terminal-sim and first-fork testing.

Its role is to teach:

- second-slot composition
- speed and response value
- fragile-die risk
- the difference between output strength and operating usefulness

It should not simply outclass Loser's Die.

It should pair well with Loser's Die because the combination gives the player a stable fallback die plus a faster fragile die.

## First-Slice Behavior

Needle Glass should make the second slot feel useful without making the build simply stronger in every way.

It should improve response, timing, and short-window play.

It should also create visible damage risk when pushed too hard.

A useful early combination is:

1. Loser's Die provides stable fallback pressure.
2. Needle Glass raises speed and response potential.
3. The player uses an accelerate manipulator on the limiting die when they want a short speed or output push.

If Loser's Die is the slowest die in the active build, accelerating Loser's Die may temporarily let the shared operating speed rise closer to the useful range opened by Needle Glass.

This should be treated as a temporary operational push or overdrive margin, not as a permanent increase to Loser's Die's real max speed.

The lesson is:

Cheap fragile dice can improve a build, but they make damage and support quality matter.

Needle Glass should introduce quality as a price-and-risk concept.

It is useful because it is affordable and fast, but its low-to-modest quality means it can run rough, demand support, and become dangerous when pushed.

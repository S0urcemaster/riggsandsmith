## Lore Identity

Black Six is a named fate die with a bad reputation in the rig scene.

It is not admired because it is elegant.

It is feared and wanted because it hits hard.

Black Six belongs to the family of dirty high-output dice: dice whose field behavior is strong, cheap, and visibly impure under load.

Its body is dark, heavy-looking, and difficult to read cleanly under inspection.

Operators describe it as if the die has already been burned once and kept working anyway.

That reputation should remain partly uncertain.

The important fact is that Black Six does not produce a clean probability bend.

It gouges at the target machine's near-future state and leaves a loud field trace behind.

Under load, Black Six tends to create short aggressive payout opportunities rather than stable long control.

It can make a weak or ordinary machine briefly feel soft, as if the next favorable state is being dragged closer by force.

That strength is also the warning.

Casino systems, floor operators, and any rig that can read field cleanliness have an easier time noticing that something unnatural happened.

In street talk, Black Six is the die someone uses when they need money now and accepts that the room may remember them afterward.

It is a bad tool for quiet farming.

It is a good tool for a hard hit, a fast exit, or a desperate escalation.

For John Riggs, Black Six should feel tempting because it solves the immediate problem Loser's Die cannot solve: weak leverage.

It should also feel dangerous because it creates exactly the kind of attention and instability that can make a small operator disappear from the scene.

## Product Identity

Black Six is a dirty high-output named die.

It should represent short-run power at visible consequence cost.

It should give the player a clear alternative to the fallback style of Loser's Die:

- Loser's Die is weak, familiar, clean, and recoverable.
- Black Six is strong, dirty, loud, and risky.

Black Six should not be a simple upgrade.

It should change the player's Hack posture from patient recovery to aggressive extraction.

## First-Slice Behavior

Black Six should make profit rise quickly when the player pushes yield or drives field energy hard.

It should also make signature, suspicion, heat, and red-zone pressure rise quickly.

Its best use should be a short greedy run with an early cash-out.

Its worst use should be repeated long operation in a weak or poorly masked rig.

In Loser's Rig, Black Six should feel powerful but poorly supported.

The player should see before or during Hack that Loser's Rig can run it, but cannot make it clean or safe.
